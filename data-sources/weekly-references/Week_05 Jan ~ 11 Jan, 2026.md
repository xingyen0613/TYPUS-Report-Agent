### Week:05 Jan, 2026

Period Covered: 5 January 2026 – 11 January 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 11st January 2026
​

Market Theme:
2026 opened with unusually high geopolitical and macro noise: a U.S. military intervention in Venezuela, a visibly cooling U.S. labour market, and an impending Supreme Court ruling on trade, all absorbed by risk assets with limited disruption as the S&P 500 still delivered roughly a 1.8% weekly gain. Venezuela holds around 17% of global proven oil reserves but currently produces less than 1% of global supply due to severely degraded infrastructure; a prospective U.S.-led rebuild worth over USD 100bn across a decade would likely extend today's low-oil, oversupplied backdrop—driven in part by China's aggressive EV pivot—and could cap energy prices for years rather than months.
​

Key News:
At the policy and product level, Florida advanced a proposal for a standalone Bitcoin reserve ahead of 2026, Ripple-linked ETFs recorded the strongest trading volumes while BTC/ETH vehicles saw outflows, and India tightened KYC requirements for crypto onboarding, highlighting the push–pull between institutionalisation and regulatory scrutiny. Morgan Stanley's filing for Bitcoin, Ethereum and Solana ETFs reinforces the continued penetration of digital assets into mainstream U.S. wealth and brokerage channels even as flows rotate within the crypto ETF complex.
​

Technicals & Macro:
The Venezuela operation generated more long-term structural questions than immediate price action: with current production so low, the near-term supply impact is muted, but a successful rehabilitation of Venezuelan output would extend the period of subdued, oversupplied energy markets and soften inflationary pressure for risk assets. On the data front, U.S. labour trends point to a "great cooling" rather than a sharp deterioration, with 2025 job growth averaging about 49k per month versus 168k in 2024 and unemployment steady around 4.4%, a mix that supports the Fed's "soft landing" narrative and justifies one to two cuts if inflation—as tested by the 13 January CPI print (expected near 2.7%)—continues to drift toward the 2% target.
​

End of the four year cycle? 2026 and beyond:
The report argues that the classic four-year halving cycle is effectively broken and that market structure is now anchored by "patient capital," with spot BTC ETFs holding around USD 140bn—roughly 7% of total supply—while new issuance has fallen below 1% per year, materially easing structural sell pressure. Bitcoin is consolidating in the USD 90k–94k range after pulling back from its October 2025 peak near USD 126k post-deleveraging, with realised volatility now comparable to high-growth tech; with the gold-to-Bitcoin ratio back at early-2024 levels, the team sees a potential "catch-up" trade where BTC reprices higher as a mainstream debasement hedge and believes new all-time highs in 2026 are well within reach even if the path is characterised by milder corrections.
​

ETH:
Ethereum enters 2026 with a sturdier fundamental base than in prior cycles, as RWAs—particularly tokenised treasuries and on-chain credit—have become a durable driver of blockspace demand and fee generation rather than purely speculative activity, and ETH still captures roughly half of the RWA segment despite some flow leakage to alternative L1s. Looking ahead, a renewed uptrend in ETH ETF inflows likely depends on macro tailwinds such as lower policy rates, healthier risk appetite and easier financial conditions, which would re-engage the marginal ETF buyer after a period of rotation and consolidation.
​

SOL:
Solana peaked early in 2025 alongside the memecoin super-cycle before selling off sharply as froth unwound and then finding technical support around USD 100, with sentiment improving mid-year on the back of SOL staking ETFs, xStocks and network upgrades that helped stabilise SOL/BTC. The desk expects altcoins—Solana included—to remain directionally tethered to Bitcoin while BTC dominance is elevated, but notes that SOL's more mature staking base, deeper institutional touchpoints and ongoing ecosystem growth leave it well-positioned to evolve from a high-beta trade into a core alt allocation once BTC stabilises or breaks higher.
​

Spot Desk:
Into year-end and early 2026, a run of firm Australian data and hawkish RBA signalling pushed markets to price a roughly 25–30% chance of a February 2026 hike and about a 75% probability of at least one move by May, keeping short-dated AUD rate differentials supportive and driving AUD/USD toward multi-week highs around 0.67 as softer U.S. cut expectations weighed on the dollar. On the desk, USDT/AUD flow frequency was heavily skewed to the offer side (about 91% of trades) even though notional volume was closer to balanced, while broader markets priced a structural discount on the bid side; USD stablecoin flow, particularly in USDT, was in line with prior weeks, USDC activity lagged the strong Q4 2025 trend, and AUDD as well as AUDM saw solid interest as AUD-pegged stablecoin rails outside traditional Australian banking. Crypto spot activity was concentrated in the majors, with BTC and SOL bid and ETH offered, and Zerocap maintains a structurally constructive view into the end of Q1 2026, including meaningful block interest in swapping BTC into IBIT via the OTC desk.
​

Derivatives Desk:
Basis has firmed modestly since the turn of the year, with the 90‑day BTC basis around 5.34% and ETH near 4.06%, levels that offer attractive carry versus risk-free rates even as options markets display a persistent put skew in both assets, signalling a preference for downside protection alongside yield-seeking positioning. The trade idea highlighted is a BTC ↔ IBIT swap structure that allows institutional investors to exchange physical BTC holdings for iShares Bitcoin Trust shares on a physically delivered basis, clearing via Zerocap's prime broker and priced off the CME CF BRRNY benchmark, enabling a shift into a regulated, exchange-traded wrapper without exiting core Bitcoin exposure.
​

What to Watch:
The macro calendar for the week focuses on AU Westpac Consumer Confidence, key U.S. inflation prints (CPI and PPI, monthly and yearly), U.S. retail sales and import/export prices, as well as China's trade balance and Australian building permits, all of which will refine the market's view on the timing and depth of 2026 rate cuts and the durability of the current risk-on stance.
