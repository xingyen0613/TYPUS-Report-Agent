### Week:23 Feb, 2026

Period Covered: 23 February 2026 – 1 March 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 2 March 2026
​

Market Theme:
Geopolitical escalation dominated the week as U.S.-Iran strikes in the Middle East triggered safe-haven demand and sharp commodity moves. BTC ETF inflows bucked a five-week outflow streak ($787M), with resilient price action suggesting a deleveraged market finding underlying spot demand. On-chain commodity perpetual swaps emerged as real-time 24/7 macro venues when traditional markets closed.
​

Key News:
U.S.-Iran strikes drove WTI crude into mid-$70s; Brent spiked aggressively before retracing; 38% market probability of Brent reaching $90 by end of March. U.S. spot BTC ETFs recorded $787M net inflows, ending a five-week outflow streak (first such reversal since the streak began); Ethereum ETFs added $80.2M. Vitalik Buterin proposed Ethereum execution layer changes to address scalability bottlenecks. Barclays exploring blockchain-based settlement tools ahead of anticipated trillion-dollar stablecoin growth. Hong Kong announced plans to issue first stablecoin issuer licenses next month. Block (Jack Dorsey's company) cut ~40% of staff, pivoting toward AI-focused operations.
​

Technicals & Macro:
DXY long-term trendline from 2011 lows broken; February failed to confirm reversal, March could prove decisive; sustained reversal would impact commodities, global liquidity, and risk asset leadership. S&P 500 shows sustained bearish divergence historically resolved only after RSI reset into oversold territory; post-NVDA earnings fatigue exposed AI complex—profit-taking, hyperscaler capex sustainability questions, and private credit concerns triggered rotation. Gold drifted back toward record levels on safe-haven demand; structurally supported if conflict broadens or shipping disruption persists; premium could fade quickly if escalation contained. Iran produces ~3.3M barrels/day; ~20% of global crude and significant LNG transit Strait of Hormuz; tanker traffic slowed, maritime incidents increased, insurance costs rising. AUD/USD opened week down ~1% (0.7103→0.7045) on weekend developments; RBA trimmed mean CPI 3.4% YoY (January)—analysts forecast high probability of rate hike to 4.1% by May meeting. Safe-haven currencies (JPY, CHF) outperformed. Friday U.S. NFP and unemployment headline key overseas prints.
​

Crypto:
BTC opened week at $67,643; sharp selloff Monday Asia open to weekly low of $62,510 as geopolitical headlines hit; implied volatility spiked, put skew blew out temporarily. Swift recovery followed: $588M in 24-hour short liquidations powered recovery; price tested $70,000 zone (recent resistance cap) before settling. As of Monday Asia hours, BTC hovering mid-$60k range, roughly unchanged from Friday. ETH mirrored the move with 11% rally on Wednesday, remaining in $1,750–$2,140 range post-liquidation. Perpetual swaps tied to oil, metals, and U.S. equities surged on Hyperliquid over the weekend—crypto-DEX functioned as 24/7 macro venue during Strait of Hormuz developments. BTC did not attract traditional safe-haven flows; acted as most liquid proxy for broader risk sentiment. Resilient ETF inflows ($787M BTC + $80.2M ETH) combined with post-liquidation price stability signal potential early indicators of market bottoming.
​

Spot Desk:
Bitcoin volumes skewed strongly bid despite macro/geopolitical volatility; persistent accumulation across various durations and algorithmic execution types. Ethereum sold against fiat crosses and block-traded at size against ETH/BTC pair. Solana remained almost exclusively bid. XRP sold uncharacteristically. Volumes concentrated in majors; altcoin breadth absent. Tentative recovery in risk appetite with continued caution in asset selection; flow dynamics persisted into Asia session with resolute aggregate bid. AUD/USD dynamics driven by RBA policy expectations and geopolitical risk-off; domestic data quieter ahead of Friday offshore prints.
​

Derivatives Desk:
BTC 3-month basis fell 90 basis points to 2.24% p.a.; ETH fell 48 basis points to 2.45%. BTC ATM implied volatility remained remarkably stable over the weekend despite significant geopolitical escalation—decoupling from spot volatility suggests market entering bottoming phase. Put skew blew out temporarily during Monday selloff then retraced on swift recovery.
