### Week:09 Feb, 2026

Period Covered: 9 February 2026 – 15 February 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 16 February 2026
​

Market Theme:
Markets stabilised following the prior week's sharp deleveraging, but structural rotation away from digital and long-duration assets persisted. Consumer participation remained weak—Coinbase's soft retail revenue and declining crypto activity on core apps signalled marginal buyers are not aggressively returning despite price bounces. The prevailing macro narrative shifted to "AI will cause disinflation," supporting duration trades while pressuring growth and software equities. Institutional flows steadied markets rather than ignited them.
​

Key News:
Coinbase reported a Q4 net loss of USD 667 million, driven by crypto revaluation and lower retail activity. Binance converted its USD 1 billion SAFU fund into Bitcoin, bringing total holdings to 15,000 BTC. Grayscale filed to convert its AAVE Trust into an ETF. U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded USD 360 million in weekly outflows. U.S. non-farm payrolls came in stronger than expected at 130,000 jobs; CPI cooled to 2.4%, reinforcing the Fed's wait-and-see stance.
​

Technicals & Macro:
Despite strong NFP and CPI data, bonds rallied as markets remain anchored to "no credible path back to rate hikes." NASDAQ showed a potential double-top formation with decelerating momentum; S&P 500 reached marginal highs at 7,002. Pronounced equity rotation from long-duration "digital stuff" toward "real stuff" and cashflow-generating assets continued—software indices declined while energy rallied. Sanae Takaichi's election victory in Japan reduced policy uncertainty; Japanese government bonds and yen firmed, providing a stabilising tailwind for global rates. AI sentiment shifted toward "AI fear" in software, with the "SaaS-pocalypse" narrative reflecting concerns about seat-based pricing losing ground to AI agents.
​

Crypto:
Bitcoin declined intraweek to USD 65,118 before closing at USD 68,832 (opened USD 70,330). Ethereum moved from USD 2,089 to close at USD 1,966. The Crypto Fear & Greed Index remained in "Extreme Fear." Altcoin pockets showed selective outperformance unseen since October's selloff, though consumer engagement remained broadly suppressed. SOL and XRP exhibited single-sided buying interest, potentially signalling sentiment floors for those assets.
​

Spot Desk:
Client flows showed tentative risk appetite reignition despite Fear and Greed remaining in Extreme Fear. Balanced selling skews in majors emerged alongside SOL and XRP single-sided buying. Smaller altcoins (AERO, FLUID, EUL) saw tactical derisking as clients consolidated into stablecoin crosses. The debasement trade continued driving consistent PAXG buying and block trades against BTC. AUD/USD rallied to 0.7147 before settling rangebound at 0.7147–0.7043, driven by RBA hawkishness. USDT/USDC maintained two-way flows against fiat crosses with robust AUDD demand and increasing EURC interest.
​

Derivatives Desk:
BTC 3-month basis at 3.50%; ETH basis at 3.03%, an inversion from the prior week. Bitcoin ATM implied volatility stabilised at a 3.5-vol-point premium above baseline, reflecting sustained caution despite consolidation. BTC options skew elevated toward puts. Trade idea: Yield Entry Notes at sub-60k BTC strikes—1-month notes at 60k strike generating approximately 1.5% yield (~18% annualised), offering value given put-heavy skew and near-yearly-low basis rates.
