### Week:02 Mar, 2026

Period Covered: 2 March 2026 – 8 March 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 10 March 2026
​

Market Theme:
Geopolitical fear bid reversed decisively as Trump indicated U.S.-Iran conflict would resolve "quickly," triggering a risk-on relief rally. Crude oil surged nearly 30% intraday to above $110/barrel before paring most gains to under $90. BTC and ETH showed resilience despite oil's ~60% intraday surge, supported by a second consecutive week of U.S. spot BTC ETF inflows (~$568M) and ~$478M in 24-hour short liquidations that drove BTC to $74,000 mid-week. Fear & Greed remained in "extreme fear" even as institutional positioning via ETF channels diverged from retail sentiment.
​

Key News:
U.S.-Israel strikes on Tehran; Trump comments signalling rapid resolution triggered week's risk reversal. U.S. spot BTC ETFs: ~$568M inflows (2nd consecutive positive week); net inflows peaked above $1.1B before reversing late week as oil-driven inflation concerns resurfaced. Strategy purchased $1.3B in Bitcoin, bringing total holdings to 738,000 BTC. Bitcoin mined supply reached 20M milestone (~1M BTC remaining for estimated 114 years). Nasdaq partnered with Kraken to expand tokenization infrastructure. Hyperliquid oil perpetual (CL-USDC) surpassed $1.2B 24-hour volume, becoming the premier venue for weekend/holiday macro trading. G7 discussed joint release of 300–400M barrels from Strategic Petroleum Reserves; OPEC+ agreed to resume incremental output increases.

Technicals & Macro:
Crude oil surged nearly 30% intraday, peaking above $110/barrel, then reversed to under $90 on Trump's conflict resolution statements. Polymarket pricing: 66% probability crude hits $100 by end of March; 74% for $85 handle; 24% for $120. February 27 PPI print reinforced restrictive policy expectations early in the week. S&P 500 and NASDAQ rebounded strongly, closing +1.44% and +2.80% respectively. IGV (Software ETF) staged a 15% recovery since the late-February lows; Anthropic's presentation de-risked the "Claude software killer" narrative. Gold's safe-haven upside capped by surging DXY and spiking 10Y yields on oil-driven inflation expectations. AUD/USD opened near 0.7045, declining through the week before rebounding +1.31% on the final day.

Crypto:
BTC and ETH outperformed traditional risk assets mid-week despite broader risk-off tone. ~$478M in 24-hour short liquidations triggered a sharp upward move, pushing BTC to $74,000 and ETH to $2,200 while Fear & Greed Index remained in "extreme fear"—highlighting divergence between retail sentiment and institutional ETF positioning. U.S. spot BTC ETF net inflows peaked above $1.1B before reversing into outflows late week as war risk and oil-driven inflation concerns returned. Week-end positioning: BTC ~$68.6k, ETH ~$2,000. Hyperliquid's CL-USDC contract established itself as primary 24/7 macro trading venue during market closures, with ETH flipping to second-most active pair after BTC.

Crypto flows heavily skewed toward bid throughout week. Majors (BTC, ETH, SOL) largely one-sided buying; altcoin flows lacked traction. Stablecoin flows moved opposite: significant USD demand as clients offered USDT/USDC. High-frequency USDT/AUD demand observed with larger block orders on offer side. AUDD actively traded and largely sold for AUD. EUR and NZD trading remained elevated. Early-week risk-off sentiment from February PPI eased into mid-week as short squeeze and ETF flows dominated; late week saw renewed caution as oil-inflation concerns resurfaced.
