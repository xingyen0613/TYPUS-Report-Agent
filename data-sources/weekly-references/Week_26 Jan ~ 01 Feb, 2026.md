### Week:26 Jan, 2026

Period Covered: 26 January 2026 – 1 February 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 2 February 2026
​

Market Theme:
Markets experienced sharp repricing across asset classes as Kevin Warsh's Fed Chair nomination triggered the largest dollar rally since May, with the greenback surging 0.9% in a single session and reversing January's slide. Gold suffered its largest intraday drop since the early 1980s, falling over 12% to break below USD 5,000/oz, while silver crashed as much as 36% intraday—the biggest collapse on record—driven by dollar strength, gamma squeeze unwind, and month-end rebalancing; despite the rout, both metals posted strong monthly gains, underscoring the prior run-up's scale. The S&P 500 slipped 0.4% but still logged its strongest monthly performance since October. Treasury yields steepened: the 2-year dropped 3bp to 3.53% while the 30-year rose 4bp to 4.89%, reflecting concerns over a less accommodative Fed balance sheet approach under Warsh's hawkish lean and skepticism of QE.
​

Key News:
Trump named Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair pick, with prediction markets shifting sharply ahead of the announcement. The SEC and CFTC launched joint "Project Crypto" to harmonise digital asset regulation and modernise market rules. Tether posted over USD 10bn in 2025 net profit while issuing more than USD 50bn of new USDT. Coinbase, Ripple and industry groups met at the White House to debate stablecoin reward treatment under the CLARITY Act. Hyperliquid recorded over USD 2.5bn in 24-hour gold and silver contract volumes as macro hedging moved on-chain. Binance pledged converting its USD 1bn SAFU fund entirely into Bitcoin.
​

Technicals & Macro:
Warsh's nomination marked a turning point for markets tilted toward "debasement trades," signalling a shift toward tighter financial conditions despite recent easing support. Fed officials remained mixed, with Waller and Miran supporting cuts while others urged patience. Risk assets broadly pulled back as traders reassessed expectations for U.S. monetary policy, Fed independence, and global inflation trajectory. Australian headline CPI rose to 3.8% annually (trimmed mean 3.3%) as rising household costs and state energy subsidy conclusion kept pressure on the RBA, with markets pricing a 72% probability of a 25bp hike on Tuesday. Policy divergence and widening yield differentials remain key currency drivers as the RBA leans hawkish while other central banks look toward easing.
​

Crypto:
Bitcoin continued trading within a wide range following October's leveraged wipeout, behaving as a high-beta risk asset during elevated macro and geopolitical uncertainty. BTC opened at USD 86,670, touched highs of USD 90,600 before a sharp sell-off accelerating Friday—as prediction markets shifted heavily toward Warsh—drove prices to lows of USD 75,700; BTC US spot ETFs recorded their third-worst month since inception at USD 1.6bn in net outflows. Ethereum mirrored weakness, opening at USD 2,816 and reaching USD 3,045 before sliding to lows of USD 2,222. Bitcoin's 24/7 trading functioned as a liquidity release valve, with two outsized spot sell orders on Saturday totalling roughly 6,000 BTC in 20 minutes, triggering cascading moves amid thin weekend liquidity and contributing to higher equity correlation over six to seven months. BTC's dip briefly pushed prices below MicroStrategy's average purchase cost (~USD 76k); however, Strategy holds roughly 713k bitcoin—all unencumbered—with significant flexibility around its USD 8.2bn convertible debt and no near-term maturities until 2027, constraining additional accumulation rather than triggering forced selling. Structurally, ongoing institutional adoption, expanding stablecoin use for cross-border settlement, and rising tokenised real-world assets should improve crypto market depth and reinforce Bitcoin's debasement hedge characteristics over time.

Spot Desk:
Client flow concentrated in majors (BTC/ETH) with continued preference toward defensive macro positioning. A PAXG (physical gold token) surge paired against stablecoins and majors highlighted rotation into gold-backed assets as precious metals dominated the global "debasement trade." SOL was a notable risk appetite bright spot, seeing persistent accumulation in a market otherwise largely absent of altcoin interest. Robust demand continued for AUDD as local stablecoin alternative to traditional banking rails, with emerging EURC interest as tactical response to recent USD weakness. AUD/USD opened at 0.6919, tested almost three-year highs at 0.7094 before partially paring gains to close at 0.6960, with strength driven by steepening domestic yield curves and firming commodity prices. Macro hedging exploded on-chain as Hyperliquid notched over USD 2.5bn in 24-hour gold and silver contract volumes. Crypto markets currently weigh Warsh's reputation as monetary hawk with QE aversion against his crypto-friendly history, describing Bitcoin favourably as a "policeman for policy."
​

Derivatives Desk:
Basis rates traded to yearly lows: BTC 90-day basis down 115bp to 3.54%, ETH down to 3.51%. BTC ATM IV rose roughly 4 vol points on 90-day contracts, with 25 delta skew remaining heavily tilted toward puts across the curve. The trade idea highlighted is a Hyperliquid Discount Note—a 6-month maturity structure where, if HYPE is above 125% of today's spot price at expiry, the investor earns 39% in USD; if below, the investor acquires HYPE at a 10% discount to current spot, offering an asymmetric risk/reward profile for tactical positioning in elevated volatility.
