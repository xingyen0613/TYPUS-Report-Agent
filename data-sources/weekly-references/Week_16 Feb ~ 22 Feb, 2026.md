### Week:16 Feb, 2026

Period Covered: 16 February 2026 – 22 February 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 23 February 2026
​

Market Theme:
Deleveraging deepened as U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded five consecutive weeks of outflows—the first such streak since March 2025. The macro backdrop shifted sharply mid-week as President Trump announced a 15% global tariff under Section 122, introducing fresh policy uncertainty despite equity markets posting their strongest weekly performance since January. The Crypto Fear & Greed Index sustained one of its longest runs in "Extreme Fear," with broad altcoin underperformance and subdued retail participation defining the week.
​

Key News:
U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs saw outflows for the fifth straight week—the first such run since March 2025. Kraken acquired tokenization platform Magna ahead of a potential IPO. President Trump announced 10% global tariffs under Section 122, subsequently raised to 15% over the weekend; Treasury guidance indicated 2026 tariff revenue broadly unchanged. Crypto firm Nexo returned to the U.S. market after a three-year regulatory absence. CME Group announced the launch of 24/7 crypto futures trading effective 29 May.
​

Technicals & Macro:
S&P 500 rose 0.7% on Friday, its strongest weekly performance since early January, before Trump's tariff announcement shifted the outlook. The dollar index fell 0.2% as USD weakened against JPY and AUD; EUR/USD rose to 1.1816; USD/JPY declined toward 154.5. The 10-year Treasury yield increased two basis points to 4.08%. Gold gained 0.8%, supported by a softer dollar and policy uncertainty. Oil eased to USD 65.95/barrel as U.S.-Iran talks resumed in Geneva. Blue Owl Capital halted redemptions in its USD 1.6 billion retail-oriented OBDC II vehicle, selling approximately USD 1.4 billion in loans near par and returning 30% of investor capital—a signal of tightening credit liquidity conditions.
​

Crypto:
BTC and ETH traded modestly lower, neither asset breaking consolidation ranges. Realized volatility compressed significantly. The 90-day SMA of top crypto assets' open interest change remained negative since October 2025, indicating sustained deleveraging. Fear & Greed Index sustained "Extreme Fear"—one of its longest such stretches. MORPHO outperformed with +40.26% YTD gains following institutional integrations and its Apollo acquisition agreement. Hyperliquid earned inclusion in Forbes Fintech 50.
​

Spot Desk:
BTC traded within a tight USD 67k–68.5k range reflecting consolidation; spot ETF inflow softening limited upside traction. Retail sentiment remained entrenched in Extreme Fear; altcoins underperformed with negative breadth. Clients showed bid-side skew on BTC and SOL; ETH largely flat. Altcoin activity lighter with modest offer-side skew. Clients increased stop-loss parameters on crypto exposure, particularly AUD pairs. AUD/USD traded within 0.7010–0.7096. USDT/AUD flows showed pronounced buy-side skew; USDT and USDC/USD flows more balanced.
​

Derivatives Desk:
BTC 3-month basis declined 16 basis points to 3.34% p.a.; ETH fell 10 basis points to 2.93%. Bitcoin ATM implied volatility traded at an elevated premium versus 2026 baselines. BTC options skew jumped approximately 2 volatility points toward puts following spot movement. Trade idea: 1-month BTC Yield Entry Note at 55k strike generating 1.5% yield (~18% annualised)—if BTC above 55k at expiry, investor receives cash plus yield; if below, investor acquires BTC at 55k plus yield in BTC.

What to Watch:
Wednesday: Australian CPI MoM/YoY, trimmed mean CPI. Thursday: US initial jobless claims. Friday: US PPI MoM, Japan retail sales YoY, Australian housing data.
