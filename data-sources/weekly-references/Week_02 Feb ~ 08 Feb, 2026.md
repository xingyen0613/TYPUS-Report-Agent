### Week:02 Feb, 2026

Period Covered: 2 February 2026 – 8 February 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 9 February 2026
​

Market Theme:
Markets experienced a sharp rotation out of risk characterised as a positioning reset rather than a structural break: the Crypto Fear & Greed Index hit 6 (lowest since June 2022) and over USD 2.6bn in crypto liquidations occurred, as crypto and growth assets saw selling pressure following years of one-way crowding while the broader index complex remained resilient. This was a re-rating of crowded risk with capital rotating into defensive and "hard economy" exposure rather than a macro system event comparable to 2022. Realised volatility ripped into highs with front-end contracts entering meaningful backwardation; however, futures basis did not implode as it does during true capitulation—the compression reflects deleveraging rather than forced, indiscriminate liquidation.
​

Key News:
BlackRock's spot Bitcoin ETF IBIT traded over USD 10bn in daily notional volume during peak volatility. The RBA delivered a 25bp rate hike to 3.85% with hawkish guidance reinforcing AUD strength and policy divergence. Senator Cynthia Lummis urged banks to embrace digital assets while stablecoin yield disputes continued to delay U.S. market structure legislation (Clarity Act). Tether expanded headcount to roughly 300 employees amid continued infrastructure growth. Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) reported one of the largest quarterly losses in U.S. public market history but reaffirmed its indefinite holding horizon. Bitcoin mining difficulty dropped 11.16%—the largest decline since the 2021 China ban—underpinned by falling prices and Winter Storm Fern forcing U.S. miners to curtail operations to support residential power grids.
​

Technicals & Macro:
NFP expected with unemployment steady and CPI around +0.3%, suggesting the Fed will remain in wait-and-see mode with rates likely unchanged unless markets destabilise materially. Political overhangs include the Warsh nomination narrative and the Clarity Act timeline, with stablecoin yield distribution remaining a key sticking point. Australian annual inflation jumped to 3.8% with the RBA's updated outlook projecting a peak of 4.2% by mid-year; the market has started pricing a more aggressive policy path, with global central bank policy divergence and widening yield differentials remaining structural tailwinds for the AUD.
​

Crypto:
Bitcoin fell from an open of USD 76,968 to lows of USD 60,000 (levels not seen since October 2024), then rebounded toward USD 70,000 and stabilised above that level by the weekend. The roughly USD 60k level corresponds with mining cost estimates for Q1 2026 (~USD 60k for lowest-cost miners), which serves as support rather than a hard floor; with approximately 20 million of 21 million BTC already circulating (only 5% incremental supply remaining versus 25% in 2018), supply dynamics are less impactful than in prior cycles, making this increasingly a demand and flow market. Ethereum slid from USD 2,270 to USD 1,747 before reclaiming USD 2,000. Cumulative losses across digital asset treasuries (DATs) are estimated around USD 25bn, led by Bitmine and Strategy with average cost bases of roughly USD 3.9k (BMNR) and USD 76k (MSTR); Strategy's Q4 report indicated BTC would need to fall to roughly USD 8k and stay there for five years before MSTR would struggle to cover its debt, providing reassurance as BTC stabilised off the roughly USD 60k zone. SOL closed around USD 87.81 (−14.2%/7D) as DeFi collateral composition shifted sharply away from meme-driven leverage—short-term pain representing the type of internal "clean-up" that typically makes the next base more durable. HYPE was the exception, gaining roughly 7% on the week and printing new weekly highs on real fundamentals: HIP-4 (prediction markets + options) arriving into fragile sentiment, Coinbase listing widening the buyer base, revenue holding around roughly USD 3m/day, expanding perps volume, and stable OI.
​

Spot Desk:
Client dynamics were characterised by guarded behaviour and suppressed risk appetite, with significant outlier selling volumes in majors (BTC/ETH) as the desk serviced large block trades into stable and fiat crosses. Volumes skewed sell-side as the "dash for cash" triggered by the Warsh pivot and volatility explosion left few stones unturned. PAXG remained a significant accumulation favourite with sizable buying against stables and block trades against BTC. In altcoins, SOL saw buying while AVAX, AERO, and Arweave (AR) faced derisking. BlackRock's IBIT traded over USD 10bn in notional on Thursday, and the 11.16% drop in mining difficulty—the largest since the 2021 China ban—underscored the scale of dislocation. AUD/USD opened at 0.6953 and surged over 100bps in the hour following the RBA's 25bp hike to 3.85%, with Governor Bullock's hawkish commentary pushing the pair to reclaim 0.7000 and consolidate near highs, ultimately closing around 0.7043 with momentum carrying into the new week.
​

Derivatives Desk:
BTC 90-day basis retraced to 3.18%. In a notable shift, ETH is commanding a premium over BTC at 3.41% despite slipping 10bps—this inversion is particularly rare as ETH's native staking yield typically exerts downward pressure on its basis relative to Bitcoin. BTC ATM IV increased roughly 3.5 vol points on 90-day contracts. The trade idea is an ETH Zero-Cost Collar expiring end of June 2026: a protective put at USD 1,500 (ensuring a minimum floor) funded by a covered call at USD 3,000 (capping upside), at zero net premium—securing present gains while retaining material upside through neutral capital deployment.
