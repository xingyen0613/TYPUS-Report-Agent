### Week:12 Jan, 2026

Period Covered: 12 January 2026 – 18 January 2026
Source: ZeroCap Weekly Crypto Market Wrap – 19 January 2026
​

Market Theme:
Global markets digested a noisy mix of macro data and political shocks with notable resilience, as small- and mid-caps led U.S. equity performance, value outpaced growth, and key domestic indicators—core CPI, producer prices, retail sales and housing—collectively sketched a benign inflation backdrop with improving activity. Core CPI slowed to 2.6% year-on-year in December, the weakest since March 2021, producer prices remained contained, retail sales beat expectations, and falling mortgage rates toward 6% helped drive a rebound in both existing and new home sales, reinforcing a "soft-landing with easing inflation" narrative even as political risk escalated.
​

Key News:
Political and policy headlines were dominated by President Trump's early-2026 agenda: military action in Venezuela, new tariff threats toward Europe and Iran, and a renewed push to replace Fed Chair Powell, with a DOJ subpoena over Fed building renovations stoking concerns about central bank independence and raising the odds of a leadership transition toward candidates such as Kevin Warsh. A pending Supreme Court ruling on tariff authority, fresh 10% U.S. tariffs on eight European countries over Greenland—with an escalation path to 25% by June—and an EU response planning up to EUR 93bn in retaliatory measures, alongside Iranian military warnings, collectively raised tail risks around trade, deficits and geopolitics, even as markets continued to assume that institutional checks and a preference for stable asset prices into the midterms would cap the fallout.
​

Technicals & Macro:
Despite the ongoing political theatre, risk assets held their ground: U.S. equities and the dollar remained higher year-to-date and rates had not materially repriced, reflecting a base case that institutions will constrain policy extremes. The week's data and price action reinforced a regime where supportive inflation dynamics, solid consumption and housing, and the absence of a sharp rates shock are allowing markets to process geopolitical noise without a wholesale de-risking, though a negative Supreme Court ruling on tariffs could still catalyse short-term volatility around trade and fiscal expectations.
​

Crypto:
Bitcoin finally broke above the USD 95k level that had capped price action since November, trading closer to USD 97k as digital assets began to catch up to the broader rally in equities and precious metals; with gold having already priced fiat debasement and geopolitical risk, BTC's prior underperformance started to reverse as investors rotated back into crypto at a relative value discount. Spot BTC ETFs saw their strongest demand since the October drawdown, with a single day net inflow of USD 760m—USD 351m from Fidelity's FBTC alone—while Ether gained about 6% midweek, lifting year-to-date performance above 13% and attracting roughly USD 130m in ETF inflows. Beneath the surface, leverage remained elevated: a sharp move earlier in the day triggered roughly USD 869m in long liquidations (around USD 229m BTC and USD 154m ETH), one of the largest events since Q4 2025, after markets rapidly repriced several macro headlines including the new tariff package and Iranian responses, yet BTC stabilised quickly near the prior breakout zone, signalling a strong underlying bid and a market structure in which structural ETF flows and asset allocation shifts are more durable than transient positioning.
​

Spot Desk:
Client flow was heavily concentrated in majors and reflected a nuanced risk-on stance: BTC and ETH saw the largest volumes with split directionality—BTC skewed to the buy side while ETH was dominated by selling—whereas SOL was exclusively bid, suggesting improving sentiment after October's broad liquidation as participants interpreted subdued futures volumes as evidence of a cleaner, de-levered market. Stablecoin flows showed balanced USDT/AUD activity, a notable pickup in USDT/USDC-to-USD off-ramping, and steady demand for AUDD as an alternative to traditional banking rails, while BTC rallied from about USD 91,013 to USD 97,924 on the week, supported by roughly USD 1.4bn in U.S. spot ETF inflows before stalling near USD 95k and experiencing a rapid drop back toward USD 91,910 in a two-hour risk-off move that coincided with a gap lower in U.S. equity futures and another parabolic leg higher in precious metals. ETH mirrored the pattern—giving back much of its gains and ending only modestly higher around USD 3,284 before sliding into Monday's Asia session—while AUD/USD traded in a remarkably tight 0.6667–0.6729 range and closed almost flat at 0.6679 despite softer-than-expected U.S. core CPI at 2.6% YoY. Beyond immediate price action, structural developments continued: CME announced February 9 listings for Cardano, Chainlink and Stellar futures as it moves toward 24/7 "always-on" trading, the CLARITY Act ran into headwinds amid a White House–Coinbase clash over stablecoin yield, BitMine Immersion Technologies committed USD 200m to MrBeast's Beast Industries (linking the largest ETH treasury to a 460m-subscriber creator ecosystem), Goldman Sachs confirmed active work on tokenisation and stablecoins, and South Korea passed a legal framework for tokenised securities; as U.S. markets return from the MLK Day holiday, attention shifts to whether Tuesday's cash open can arrest the current risk slide.
​

Derivatives Desk:
Basis softened over the week, with the 90‑day BTC basis falling about 65 bps to 4.69% and ETH down to 3.78%, compressing the forward premium even as structural ETF flows and spot interest remain healthy. Against this backdrop, the desk highlights a June 2026 BTC USD 120k/150k bull-call spread costing roughly 2.7% (about USD 2,500 per unit) with up to 12x premium payoff if realised, built on three observed conditions: long-dated at-the-money implied volatility is depressed after a sluggish 2025, put-heavy skew makes out-of-the-money upside calls relatively cheap, and lower basis reduces the cost-of-carry embedded in option pricing, collectively creating an attractive entry point for levered upside exposure.
​

What to Watch:
The upcoming data calendar includes China GDP and Canadian inflation on Monday, U.K. unemployment on Tuesday, U.K. inflation and U.S. API crude inventories on Wednesday, Australian unemployment, U.S. core PCE (MoM, including back data for October and November), U.S. GDP, Japanese inflation and the BOJ rate decision on Thursday, and U.K. retail sales on Friday; together these prints will inform how far markets can push the current risk-on rotation before tighter policy or growth disappointments force a reassessment.
