Market Pulse & TVL

January 2026 delivered a textbook risk-asset rotation: Bitcoin climbed from around $88k to touch $97k by mid-month on the back of renewed spot ETF inflows and institutional re-engagement, only to reverse sharply into month-end as macro crosscurrents—including fresh tariff threats, geopolitical noise around Venezuela and Iran, and concerns over Fed independence—triggered broad deleveraging across digital assets. Ethereum mirrored the pattern, rallying from roughly $3.0k toward $3.4k before giving back gains to close near $2.5k, while altcoins including SOL, SUI and XRP experienced even steeper drawdowns in the final two weeks as leverage unwound and risk appetite cooled.

Against this backdrop, Typus TVL adjusted from approximately $5.2M at year-end to $4.7M by late January, a decline of roughly 10% that closely tracked the performance of SUI—the underlying network on which Typus operates—which fell approximately 19% from $1.43 to $1.16 over the same period. The TVL movement reflected both the broader market reset and the natural repricing of SUI-denominated assets rather than protocol-specific outflows. Critically, the platform maintained operational stability throughout the correction, with Options TVL holding around $4.7M as users continued to engage with structured products even as volatility spiked. The TVL trajectory mirrors the healthy consolidation seen across DeFi ecosystems built on high-performance L1s: assets repricing in response to macro uncertainty while underlying infrastructure and user behaviour remain intact.

Performance Deep Dive

Perps Platform

January marked a strategic milestone: the official relaunch of Typus Perps following completion of a comprehensive security audit and infrastructure overhaul. After pausing operations in late 2025 to address technical and security enhancements, the platform executed a phased return: TLP Pre-deposit opened on January 28, allowing liquidity providers to position capital ahead of the formal trading launch scheduled for February 4. This deliberate, staged approach prioritised platform stability and liquidity depth over speed to market, with the team conducting final system validation and user onboarding during the pre-deposit window. The late-month launch meant January captured only the initial early liquidity deployment phase, establishing the foundation for full-scale trading operations in early February. The relaunch positions Typus to recapture market share in decentralised perpetuals as conditions stabilise and risk appetite returns.

Total Value Locked

Platform-wide TVL stood at roughly $4.7M at month-end, down about 10% from December's $5.2M in a move consistent with the sharp drawdown in underlying collateral prices—Bitcoin fell around 8% and Ethereum shed close to 15% over the same period—and a broader flight to quality as traders reduced exposure ahead of macro event risk. The decline was orderly and reflected market-driven repricing rather than systemic withdrawal, with the protocol's multi-product architecture continuing to attract capital across DOV, SAFU and the relaunched Perps offering.

Options TVL

Options TVL—comprising DOV and SAFU positions—totalled approximately $4.7M, a decline of roughly 10% month-over-month that tracked the overall platform trend. DOV TVL adjusted to around $3.6M while SAFU holdings moderated to approximately $554k, both cooling in response to lower crypto prices and reduced speculative appetite. Importantly, the structured-product user base remained engaged: vaults continued to execute regular auctions, bidders participated across strikes and tenors, and the protocol's automated compounding and dynamic fill-rate mechanisms kept product functionality smooth even as market conditions tightened.

Fees & Volume

The Perps platform was officially relaunched on January 28, followed by a one-week pre-deposit phase as part of the planned rollout, allowing liquidity to position ahead of trading. Full trading will commence on February 4, after which fee and volume metrics will begin to reflect more representative, steady-state trading activity on the platform.
