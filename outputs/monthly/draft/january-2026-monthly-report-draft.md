## Market Pulse & TVL

January 2026 delivered a textbook risk-asset rotation: Bitcoin climbed from around $88k to touch $97k by mid-month on the back of renewed spot ETF inflows and institutional re-engagement, only to reverse sharply into month-end as macro crosscurrents—including fresh tariff threats, geopolitical noise around Venezuela and Iran, and concerns over Fed independence—triggered broad deleveraging across digital assets. Ethereum mirrored the pattern, rallying from roughly $3.0k toward $3.4k before giving back gains to close near $2.5k, while altcoins including SOL, SUI and XRP experienced even steeper drawdowns in the final two weeks as leverage unwound and risk appetite cooled.

Against this backdrop, Typus TVL adjusted from approximately $5.2M at year-end to $4.7M by late January, a decline of roughly 10% that reflected the broader market reset rather than protocol-specific outflows. Critically, the platform maintained operational stability throughout the correction, with Options TVL holding around $4.7M as users continued to engage with structured products even as volatility spiked. The TVL trajectory mirrors the healthy consolidation seen across DeFi: assets repricing in response to macro uncertainty while underlying infrastructure and user behaviour remain intact.

## Performance Deep Dive

**Perps Platform**

January marked a strategic milestone: the official relaunch of Typus Perps following completion of a comprehensive security audit and infrastructure overhaul. After pausing operations in late 2025 to address technical and security enhancements, the platform executed a phased return: TLP Pre-deposit opened on January 28, allowing liquidity providers to position capital ahead of the formal trading launch scheduled for February 4. This deliberate, staged approach prioritised platform stability and liquidity depth over speed to market, with the team conducting final system validation and user onboarding during the pre-deposit window. By month-end, Perps TVL reached approximately $3.3k as early liquidity providers began committing capital, establishing the foundation for full-scale trading operations in early February. The relaunch positions Typus to recapture market share in decentralised perpetuals as conditions stabilise and risk appetite returns.

**Total Value Locked**

Platform-wide TVL stood at roughly $4.7M at month-end, down about 10% from December's $5.2M in a move consistent with the sharp drawdown in underlying collateral prices—Bitcoin fell around 8% and Ethereum shed close to 15% over the same period—and a broader flight to quality as traders reduced exposure ahead of macro event risk. The decline was orderly and reflected market-driven repricing rather than systemic withdrawal, with the protocol's multi-product architecture continuing to attract capital across DOV, SAFU and the relaunched Perps offering.

**Options TVL**

Options TVL—comprising DOV and SAFU positions—totalled approximately $4.7M, a decline of roughly 10% month-over-month that tracked the overall platform trend. DOV TVL adjusted to around $3.6M while SAFU holdings moderated to approximately $554k, both cooling in response to lower crypto prices and reduced speculative appetite. Importantly, the structured-product user base remained engaged: vaults continued to execute regular auctions, bidders participated across strikes and tenors, and the protocol's automated compounding and dynamic fill-rate mechanisms kept product functionality smooth even as market conditions tightened.

**Fees & Volume**

Perps fee and volume metrics reflect the late-month timing of the platform relaunch: with TLP Pre-deposit opening on January 28 and full trading scheduled for February 4, the month captured only the initial pre-deposit phase rather than active trading activity. Minimal notional volume of approximately $278 and protocol fees of roughly $0.25 represent early testing and system validation rather than steady-state performance. These baseline figures will scale materially once trading commences in February, supported by deepening liquidity, expanded user onboarding and upcoming initiatives—including trading competitions with dedicated prize pools—designed to drive higher participation and demonstrate the platform's enhanced infrastructure under live market conditions.

## User Engagement

**User Activity**

Core product MAU reached 342 active users in January, a modest 5% decline from December's 360 that reflects the broader risk-off environment and seasonal slowdown following the year-end holidays. Importantly, the user base demonstrated resilience: despite sharp market corrections and the temporary Perps relaunch learning curve, engagement with Options vaults and SAFU strategies remained steady, with 320 users interacting with Typus v2 products and 22 early adopters testing the relaunched Perps platform. The retention of an active, quality-focused user base through a volatile month underscores the stickiness of Typus structured products and positions the protocol well for re-acceleration as market sentiment improves.

Total unique users interacting with core product sections reached 23,047, adding 48 new users during the month. While new-user acquisition slowed in line with sector-wide trends, the protocol maintained steady organic growth and preserved its reputation as a destination for sophisticated DeFi participants seeking yield and leveraged exposure beyond simple staking or farming.
