## Market Pulse & TVL

March 2026 was governed by a single macro variable: the trajectory of the US-Israel-Iran conflict and whether it would escalate further or move toward resolution. Throughout the month, risk sentiment lurched between relief rallies—triggered by signals of potential ceasefire or diplomatic progress—and sharp sell-offs that followed each new escalation headline. Crude oil maintained elevated pressure above $100 per barrel for the majority of the month, US 10-year yields spiked to their highest levels since mid-2025, and the Fear & Greed Index spent the entire month in Extreme Fear territory, logging over 46 consecutive days below 15 by month-end. The S&P 500 logged its fifth consecutive weekly decline, breaking below its 200-day moving average for the first time since May 2025.

Against this backdrop, crypto markets showed notable divergence. Bitcoin opened at roughly $67k, briefly touched $74k mid-month on the back of a geopolitical relief rally and a sharp short squeeze, before retreating to close near $68.3k—broadly flat at approximately +2% for the month. Ethereum outperformed, gaining approximately 7% to close above $2,100. SUI declined roughly 3% over the same period.

Typus TVL adjusted to approximately $1.9M (-45%) as the sustained uncertainty environment weighed on DeFi participation broadly and dampened structured product appetite. The decline was most pronounced in Options vaults, reflecting reduced risk appetite for yield strategies during an extended period of extreme market fear—an environment where market participants consistently shifted toward stablecoin positioning and lower-risk instruments. SAFU TVL, by contrast, grew approximately 21% as users directed capital toward more conservative yield strategies. Because Typus protocol deposits are predominantly SUI-denominated, the USD value of TVL naturally moves in tandem with SUI's market price; the broader macro reset compounded this structural sensitivity. The TVL trajectory is consistent with DeFi-wide patterns observed across the ecosystem during this period of macro stress.

## Performance Deep Dive

**Perps Platform**

Monthly notional volume reached approximately $7.7M, more than doubling from February's ~$3.3M (+134% month-over-month). The surge was driven by the launch of xStock—Typus' expansion into US Equity perpetuals—which introduced multiple new trading pairs and meaningfully expanded the addressable market for the platform's leveraged trading offering. Fee revenue grew in line with the volume expansion, reflecting healthy platform activity. The performance underscores the strategic value of product diversification: even as geopolitical uncertainty weighed on overall crypto sentiment, xStock pairs attracted fresh trading activity from participants seeking leveraged exposure to US equity markets through on-chain infrastructure.

**Total Value Locked**

Platform-wide TVL stood at approximately $1.9M at month-end, down from approximately $3.5M in February. The decline reflected the broader risk-off environment characterised by over 46 consecutive days of extreme fear, declining DeFi participation across the ecosystem, and reduced appetite for structured product strategies during periods of sustained macro stress. The protocol maintained full operational continuity throughout the month with no disruptions to vault execution, trading operations or user access.

**Options TVL**

Options TVL—comprising DOV and SAFU positions—totalled approximately $1.8M, adjusting from approximately $3.2M in February. DOV TVL moderated significantly as participants reduced exposure to options strategies in favour of more defensive positioning, consistent with the sector-wide shift toward lower-risk instruments during the conflict-driven uncertainty. SAFU TVL grew approximately 21%, demonstrating that users seeking conservative yield strategies remained engaged with the platform. Vault execution continued without disruption, with regular auctions clearing across active strike ranges throughout the month.

**Fees & Volume**

Notional volume reached approximately $7.7M for the month—a 134% increase from February—driven primarily by xStock adoption. Fee revenue expanded alongside volume. This growth trajectory demonstrates the platform's capacity to generate meaningful trading activity even during adverse macro conditions, when an expanded product suite provides participants with alternatives to traditional crypto-only exposure.
